About AutoGlob

Time & Road — Stories Behind The Machines

AutoGlob exists for one reason: most car writing either drowns you in spec sheets or oversells you on hype. We wanted something in between — stories about machines that treat the reader as someone who actually cares how and why things were built the way they were.

We cover three things, on purpose. Supercars, because the engineering at the extreme edge of what's possible tells you something about where the whole industry is headed. Classics, because the cars that have already proven themselves worth preserving deserve more than a nostalgic caption. And forgotten brands, because some of the most interesting stories in automotive history belong to companies that didn't survive to tell them.

What we actually do

Every piece we publish tries to answer a simple question: why does this machine matter, beyond the numbers? That means original reporting where we can get it, careful research where we can't, and an editorial line that stays skeptical of both manufacturer press releases and internet hype cycles in equal measure.

What we don't do

  • We don't accept payment in exchange for favorable coverage.
  • We don't publish spec-sheet rewrites disguised as reviews.
  • We don't pretend every new release is the most important car ever built.
